Malai Kulfi

Matka kulfi | Malai kulfi | Matka kesar pista kulfi a popular frozen traditional indian ice cream recipe prepared with milk cream and topped with dry fruits. an ideal indian dessert recipe which is denser and creamier than compared to the normal ice creams. there are several ways and flavours to the kulfi recipe, and matka malai kulfi recipe is one such variety.

Falooda

This adaptation of a Persian dessert was brought to India by the Mughals. A rich drink, Falooda is vermicelli mixed with milk, almonds, pistachios, a bit of rose syrup and the key ingredient -- sabza or basil seeds -- topped up with two scoops of ice cream. Refreshing, rosy, energizing, it's a great pick-me-up on a hot day. Badshah, at 152/156 LT Marg, Opp. Crawford Market. Tel: +91 (0) 22 2342 1943, has a reputation for their falooda.

Kheer

Kheer, Payasa, Payasam or Phirni, is a type of pudding from the Indian subcontinent, made by boiling milk, sugar, and rice, although rice may be substitute with one of the following: bulgar wheat, millet, tapioca, vermicelli, or sweet corn.It is typically flavored with desiccated coconut, cardamom, raisins, saffron, cashews, pistachios, almonds, or other dry fruits and nuts. It is typically served during a meal or as a dessert.

Modak

Ukadiche modaks are the traditional ones and are deemed to be the tastiest of them all. Known to be Lord Ganesha’s favourite food (that’s why the prasad), udakdiche modaks are made from rice flour with a stuffing of jaggery and fresh coconut. It’s like an Indian dumpling with a sweet filling. While it sounds very simple, it’s actually quite complex and you need to be extremely proficient at it, to get them right. It takes patience and a lot of practice to get your modaks right.
